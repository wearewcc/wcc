Grace Hopper

“Leadership is a two-way street, loyalty up and loyalty down. Respect for one's superiors; care for one's crew.”

Grace Hopper was an American computer scientist and United States Navy Rear Admiral. Hopper was one of the first programmers of the Harvard Mark I computer in 1944, invented the first compiler for a computer programming language, and was one of those who popularized the idea of machine-independent programming languages which led to the development of COBOL, one of the first high-level programming languages.

Interesting Facts

  • Owing to her accomplishments and her naval rank, Hopper is sometimes referred to as Amazing Grace
  • The U.S. Navy guided-missile destroyer USS Hopper is named for her
  • Cited with influencing the computer term bug, after finding an actual moth in the Mark II computer.
